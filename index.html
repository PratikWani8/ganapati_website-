Manache Pach Ganpati (Pune)

1. Shri Kasba Ganpati:

The first and most historically significant of the Manache Ganpati, holding a special place in the city's devotion and history.

2. Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati:

Also known as the second Manache Ganpati, it is established at the site of the popular Tambdi Jogeshwari temple.

3. Guruji Talim Ganpati:

The third most honored Ganpati, it has a strong connection to the local history of Pune.

4. Tulshibaug Ganpati:

Known as the fourth Manache Ganpati, it symbolizes the perseverance of Pune's small business owners and hawkers.

5. Kesri Wada Ganpati:

The fifth and final of the Manache Ganpati, it completes the important set of venerated Ganesh mandals in Pune.

About Lord Ganapati

Lord Ganesh (Ganesha, Ganapati, Vinayaka) is the Hindu God of wisdom, prosperity, and remover of obstacles. He is the son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, with an elephant head, big belly, and mouse as his vehicle. His broken tusk symbolizes sacrifice and wisdom.

He is worshipped first in every puja for success and good fortune. His favorite sweet is modak, and his main festival is Ganesh Chaturthi.
